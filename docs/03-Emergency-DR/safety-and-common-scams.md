# üö® Safety & Common Scams (2026 Update)

**TL;DR:** China is extremely safe from physical crime, but "Social Engineering" scams target foreigners in tourist hubs. If a stranger is "too friendly" and suggests a specific location, it's a trap.

**Prerequisites:**
- **App:** Alipay/WeChat (to check prices of things).
- **Mental Model:** "Nothing is free in a tourist zone."

## üìã The Runbook

### 1. The "Tea Ceremony / Art Student" Protocol
- **The Setup:** A group of students or a friendly couple approaches you at The Bund, People's Square, or East Nanjing Road. They want to "practice English" or show you a "local art gallery."
- **The Trap:** They take you to a private room for tea or art. You are presented with a bill for 5,000 - 20,000 RMB.
- **Verification:** If the invite comes within the first 5 minutes of meeting a stranger in a high-traffic zone, **it is a scam.**
- **Action:** Politely say "No, thank you" and keep walking.

### 2. The "Broken Meter" Taxi Protocol
- **The Setup:** A driver at the airport or station says "Meter broken, flat price 400 RMB."
- **Verification:** Check the **DiDi** app for the real price. It's usually 60-100 RMB.
- **Action:** Only use the **Official Taxi Queue**. If a driver refuses the meter, get out immediately and take a photo of their ID card on the dashboard.

### 3. The "QR Code Overlay" Hack
- **The Setup:** Scammers put their own QR code sticker over a legitimate merchant's code.
- **Verification:** In Alipay/WeChat, check the **recipient's name** before hitting "Confirm." It should match the shop name or the staff's uniform.
- **Action:** If the name is a random individual (e.g., "*Mr. Zhang"), double-check with the staff.
