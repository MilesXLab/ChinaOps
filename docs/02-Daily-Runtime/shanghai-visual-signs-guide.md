## ğŸš¨ Fallback (Plan B)

### If you see a sign you don't understand and it looks critical:
1. **The "Scan & Patch" SOP:** Open Google Translate. Hold the camera up to the sign. It will "AR-overlay" the translation.
2. **Contextual Logic:** If the sign is Red and has an exclamation mark (!), it's a **Blocker**. Stop and verify before proceeding.
3. **The "Local Observer" Trick:** Watch what the locals are doing. If they are all swiping their phones at a specific gate, it's a **System Requirement**.

## ğŸ’¡ TechDad's Tips

- **The "Red Stamp" (GÅngzhÄng):** In China, a red circular stamp is the only thing that makes a document "Official." An ink signature alone is often rejected.
- **Exit Numbers:** In Shanghai Metro, a station might have 20 exits. **Always** check the map for the specific Exit Number (e.g., "Exit 4") before you go up the escalator.
- **Color Coding:** 
  - **Green:** Safety / Exit.
  - **Red:** Warning / Metro entrance.
  - **Blue:** Information / Direction.
- **Waimai Scooters:** Watch for the blue (Ele.me) or yellow (Meituan) uniforms. They are the fastest and most dangerous "objects" in the environment.
