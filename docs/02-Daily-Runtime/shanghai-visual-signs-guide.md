# Shanghai Visual Signs & Symbols Guide

**Last Updated:** Jan 2026 | **Author:** TechDadShanghai

### Context
Shanghai uses a mix of international symbols and Chinese-specific signage. This visual guide helps you quickly identify essential facilities and services without needing to read Chinese characters.

---

## 1. Metro & Transportation Signs

### Shanghai Metro Logo
- **Symbol:** Red circle with white "M" and Chinese characters "上海地铁"
- **Where:** All metro station entrances, exits, and platforms
- **What it means:** Official Shanghai Metro entrance/station
- **Tip:** Look for the red "M" - it's visible from blocks away

### Metro Line Numbers
- **Symbol:** Colored circles with numbers (e.g., Line 1 = Red, Line 2 = Green)
- **Where:** Station platforms, train doors, route maps
- **What it means:** Identifies which metro line you're on
- **Key Lines:**
  - Line 2 (Green): Hongqiao Airport ↔ Pudong Airport
  - Line 10 (Purple): Hongqiao Railway/Airport ↔ Tourist areas
  - Line 1 (Red): North-South main artery

### Exit Signs (出口)
- **Chinese:** 出口 (Chūkǒu)
- **Symbol:** Arrow with "EXIT" or numbered exits (1号口, 2号口)
- **Where:** Inside metro stations
- **Tip:** Exit numbers are crucial - save your destination's exit number to avoid long walks

### Taxi Stand (出租车)
- **Chinese:** 出租车 (Chūzūchē)
- **Symbol:** Yellow/green taxi icon with Chinese characters
- **Where:** Airport, train stations, major buildings
- **What to look for:** Official taxi ranks have barriers and queue lines
- **Colors:** 
  - Teal/Turquoise taxis = Official Shanghai taxis
  - Yellow taxis = Also official
  - Avoid: Unmarked black cars

## 2. Restroom & Hygiene Facilities

### Public Toilets (卫生间/洗手间)
- **Chinese:** 卫生间 (Wèishēngjiān) or 洗手间 (Xǐshǒujiān)
- **International Symbol:** Standard male/female icons
- **Where:** Metro stations, malls, parks, tourist areas
- **Additional Signs:**
  - 男 (Nán) = Men
  - 女 (Nǚ) = Women
  - WC = Also commonly used

### Accessible/Family Toilet
- **Symbol:** Wheelchair icon or parent+child icon
- **Chinese:** 无障碍卫生间 (Wúzhàng'ài wèishēngjiān)
- **Where:** Larger metro stations, malls, airports
- **Features:** Usually includes baby changing table, more space

---

## 3. Mother & Baby Facilities

### Nursing Room (母婴室)
- **Chinese:** 母婴室 (Mǔyīng shì)
- **Symbol:** Mother holding baby icon (usually pink/purple)
- **Where:** High-end malls (IFC, IAPM, K11), airports, major metro stations
- **Features:** Private nursing area, changing table, hot water, sometimes microwave
- **Quality Tier:**
  - *** (Three Stars) Premium malls (IFC, Reel, IAPM)
  - ** (Two Stars) Standard malls
  - * (One Star) Metro stations (basic facilities)

### Baby Changing Table
- **Symbol:** Baby on changing table icon
- **Chinese:** 婴儿护理台 (Yīng'ér hùlǐ tái)
- **Where:** Many public restrooms in malls and stations
- **Tip:** Often found in accessible/family toilets

## 4. Emergency & Safety Signs

### Hospital/Medical (医院)
- **Chinese:** 医院 (Yīyuàn)
- **Symbol:** Red cross or "H" sign
- **Where:** Hospitals, clinics, pharmacies
- **International Hospitals:** Look for "International Department" in English

### Police (警察)
- **Chinese:** 警察 (Jǐngchá) or 公安 (Gōng'ān)
- **Symbol:** Police badge, blue uniform
- **Where:** Police stations, security checkpoints
- **Emergency Number:** 110

### Fire Exit (安全出口)
- **Chinese:** 安全出口 (Ānquán chūkǒu)
- **Symbol:** Green running person with arrow
- **Where:** All buildings, malls, metro stations
- **Standard:** International green exit sign

### First Aid (急救)
- **Chinese:** 急救 (Jíjiù)
- **Symbol:** White cross on green background
- **Where:** Stations, malls, public venues

## 5. Shopping & Services

### Information Desk (咨询台/服务台)
- **Chinese:** 咨询台 (Zīxún tái) or 服务台 (Fúwù tái)
- **Symbol:** "i" icon or question mark
- **Where:** Malls, stations, tourist areas
- **Tip:** Staff often speak basic English in tourist areas

### ATM (自动取款机)
- **Chinese:** ATM or 自动取款机 (Zìdòng qǔkuǎn jī)
- **Symbol:** Standard ATM icon
- **Where:** Banks, convenience stores, metro stations
- **Tip:** Look for "UnionPay" logo for international cards

### Convenience Store
- **Brands:** FamilyMart (全家), 7-Eleven, Lawson (罗森)
- **Symbol:** Bright store logos, usually open 24/7
- **Where:** Every few blocks in urban areas
- **Services:** ATM, hot water, restrooms (sometimes), snacks

## 6. Food & Dining

### Restaurant/Dining (餐厅)
- **Chinese:** 餐厅 (Cāntīng)
- **Symbol:** Fork and knife, or chopsticks icon
- **Where:** Malls, streets, food courts

### Food Court (美食广场)
- **Chinese:** 美食广场 (Měishí guǎngchǎng)
- **Where:** Usually basement or top floor of malls
- **Tip:** Great for variety and seeing food before ordering

### No Outside Food (禁止外带食品)
- **Chinese:** 禁止外带食品 (Jìnzhǐ wàidài shípǐn)
- **Symbol:** Food icon with red X
- **Where:** Some malls, Disney, museums
- **Note:** Water bottles usually OK

---

## 7. Access & Restrictions

### Elevator (电梯)
- **Chinese:** 电梯 (Diàntī)
- **Symbol:** Up/down arrows
- **Types:**
  - 直梯 (Zhí tī) = Vertical elevator
  - 扶梯 (Fú tī) = Escalator

### Escalator Direction
- **Up:** 上 (Shàng) with up arrow
- **Down:** 下 (Xià) with down arrow
- **Tip:** Stand on right, walk on left (Shanghai convention)

### No Entry (禁止进入)
- **Chinese:** 禁止进入 (Jìnzhǐ jìnrù)
- **Symbol:** Red circle with white horizontal bar
- **Where:** Restricted areas, staff-only zones

### No Smoking (禁止吸烟)
- **Chinese:** 禁止吸烟 (Jìnzhǐ xīyān)
- **Symbol:** Cigarette with red X
- **Where:** Most indoor public spaces
- **Note:** Shanghai has strict indoor smoking bans

---

## 8. Parking & Vehicles

### Parking (停车场)
- **Chinese:** 停车场 (Tíngchē chǎng)
- **Symbol:** "P" sign
- **Where:** Malls, buildings, underground
- **Tip:** Usually paid parking, scan QR code to pay

### Bicycle Parking (自行车停放处)
- **Chinese:** 自行车停放处 (Zìxíngchē tíngfàng chù)
- **Symbol:** Bicycle icon
- **Where:** Metro stations, malls, streets
- **Shared Bikes:** Look for designated parking zones (white boxes on ground)

## TechDad's Tips

### Quick Recognition Strategy
1. **Color Coding:**
   - Red = Metro, emergency, prohibited
   - Green = Exit, safety, go
   - Blue = Information, services
   - Yellow/Orange = Warning, caution

2. **Universal Symbols:**
   - Shanghai uses many international symbols
   - When in doubt, look for icons rather than text
   - Most signs in tourist areas have English

3. **Photo Translation:**
   - Use Google Translate camera mode for unknown signs
   - Take photos of important signs (hotel, metro exit) for reference

### Essential Signs to Memorize

**Top 5 Most Useful:**
1. 出口 (Exit) - You'll see this everywhere
2. 卫生间 (Toilet) - Essential for comfort
3. 地铁 (Metro) - Your main transport
4. 出租车 (Taxi) - Backup transport
5. 母婴室 (Nursing Room) - For parents

**Safety Signs:**
1. 安全出口 (Emergency Exit)
2. 医院 (Hospital)
3. 警察 (Police)
4. 急救 (First Aid)
