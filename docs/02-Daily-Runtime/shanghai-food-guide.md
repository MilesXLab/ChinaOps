## ğŸš¨ Fallback (Plan B)

### If you are in a neighborhood with no English menus and no signal:
1. **The "Display Cabinet" Strategy:** Many local eateries have their ingredients or dishes in a display case. Point and gesture.
2. **Chain Nodes:** Look for **Saizeriya** (Italian), **Ajisen Ramen**, or **McDonald's**. They have predictable quality and picture-based ordering.
3. **The "Hema" Buffet:** Go to a **Hema (Freshippo)** supermarket. They have a massive cooked-food section where you can pick exactly what you want and pay via Alipay.

## ğŸ’¡ TechDad's Tips

- **The "Elders" Rule:** If you see a line of local retirees at a hole-in-the-wall at 8:00 AM, the food is authentic, cheap, and safe.
- **Hot Tea:** Most traditional places provide free hot tea. It's the local "antiseptic" and much better than cold soda.
- **Hairy Crab Season:** If visiting in Oct/Nov, focus your spending here. It's the highlight of the Shanghai culinary calendar.
- **Table Manners:** Don't stick your chopsticks vertically in a bowl of rice (it's a funeral ritual). Lay them across the bowl or on the rest provided.
